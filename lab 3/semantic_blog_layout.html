Significance of Dashahara

Dashahara, also known as Dussehra, is a significant Hindu festival celebrated across India. It marks the triumph of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana, symbolizing the victory of good over evil.

Legend of Dashahara

According to the epic Ramayana, Lord Rama, with the help of his brother Lakshmana and devotee Hanuman, defeated Ravana and rescued his wife Sita from his captivity. The festival celebrates this victory and the triumph of righteousness.

Celebrations and Traditions

Dashahara is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm across India. People burn effigies of Ravana, symbolizing the destruction of evil. The festival is also marked by traditional dances, music, and worship.
